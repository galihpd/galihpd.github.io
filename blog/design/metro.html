Metro, the most mysterious design style

By Galih Prakoso +2
November 23 2022

Windows 8 Source:Wikipedia

Metro Design Style is a design style popularized by software company Microsoft. This design style is used in almost all Microsoft software products from 2012 to the present(Last on Windows 10).

This design style bears similarities to the Flat Design Style, which was inspired by the Swiss Design Style of the 1940s to 1950s. The focus of Swiss Design itself is on the use of grid, sans-serif typefaces. Microsoft actually used the Flat Design style first in 1995 at which time the Flat Design style was used in their software product, Encarta 95.

The metro design style relies more on lots of simple grid layouts and the use of sans-serif typefaces. Has a main focus, namely increasing user productivity by displaying a neat and more attractive design.

The Metro Usage

Xbox System Software From:Xbox.com

The Metro Design style can be found in the interface design of software such as Windows Phone, Xbox System Software, and almost any other Microsoft product.

The Man Behind It

Bill Flora. From: General Assembly

An important figure in the development of the Metro design style was Bill Flora. The design principles he founded today still illuminate millions of screens around the world every day. He is an award-winning design director and strategist with 30 years of experience in leading teams and shipping products. During his two decades with Microsoft, he led design efforts for Xbox, Windows Media Center, Zune, and Encarta, and was the design force behind Microsoft "Metro" design language
